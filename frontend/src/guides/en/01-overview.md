# About This App

This app is a household finance tool for managing your money around a personal balance sheet, not only around daily expense entry. Instead of only showing "how much did I spend this month?", it treats cash, bank deposits, credit cards, lending, borrowing, crypto assets, fixed assets, and remaining budgets as one set of books.

Internally, the app uses double-entry bookkeeping. For everyday entry, however, you do not need to think in debits and credits. Simple entry lets you choose an expense, income, transfer, or loan-related transaction, and the app creates the required journal entry behind the scenes.

## What This App Manages

| Area | Main page | What you can do |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Daily entry | Input | Record expenses, income, transfers, lending/borrowing, and CSV imports |
| Ledger review | Ledger | Review entries in simple view or double-entry view |
| Financial state | Financial Statements | See assets, liabilities, net worth, profit/loss, and financial health |
| Budgeting | Overview, Budget Settings | Create purpose-based virtual buckets on top of cash and deposits, and manage available balances |
| Reconciliation | Financial Statements -> Trial Balance | Compare actual balances with book balances, and check credit cards or unknown funds |
| Special assets | Crypto, Savings, Loan Management | Manage wallet balances, savings goals, lending, and borrowing |

## Basic Concepts

In this app, you first create accounts. Accounts are the real items that appear directly in the ledger, such as bank accounts, credit cards, salary, food expenses, and loans receivable. Each account has one of five types: asset, liability, equity, income, or expense.

Budget categories are different from accounts. A budget category is a virtual purpose-based bucket layered on top of real assets such as cash and bank deposits. For example, if your bank balance is 100,000 yen, you can view 30,000 yen as food, 20,000 yen as entertainment, and 50,000 yen as savings.

## First Setup Steps

1. In Settings, create the bank accounts, credit cards, income accounts, and main expense accounts you use.
2. In Initial Balance entry, register the account and card balances at your starting point.
3. In Budget Settings, create budget categories such as food, entertainment, and savings.
4. If needed, create budget filters that automatically distribute income across budget categories.
5. Record daily transactions from the Input page.
6. Occasionally use the Trial Balance page to check whether actual balances match the ledger.

## How To Choose Pages

For daily use, the Overview and Input pages may be enough. Use Settings -> Budget Settings when reviewing budgets, Financial Statements -> Trial Balance when balances do not match, and the subpages under Financial Statements when you want a closer look at cards, loans, or other details.

If you understand double-entry bookkeeping, the multi-line entry form and the ledger's double-entry view give you finer control. If you do not, the app is designed so routine management still works through simple entry and trial balance checks.
